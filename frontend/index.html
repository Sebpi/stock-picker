            Colour key: Green ≥ 70 · Amber 45–69 · Red < 45

            Sub-scores (small text below badges): RSI — overbought > 70, oversold < 30  ·  52w% — where price sits in its 52-week range  ·  SMA — % above/below 50-day moving average  ·  FCF — free cash flow yield (> 8% is strong)

            How to read a stock: Start with ⊕ as a headline — ≥ 65 is a solid base. Then look at MoS: a positive margin confirms the model thinks the stock is trading below its intrinsic worth. The strongest setups combine V ≥ 65, M ≥ 60, Q ≥ 60, and a positive MoS.

4-Week Backtest

          
Replays the sentiment scoring model against 4 weeks of historical data to measure directional accuracy. Uses VIX, S&P momentum, beta, and fundamentals (news excluded — historical headlines unavailable).

        

        

          
        

      

      
      

        
How to read the results

        

          

            🎯
            Accuracy %
            
How often the model predicted the correct direction (up or down) for the next day. 50% = coin flip. Above 60% = model has an edge for that stock.

          

          

            📏
            Variance ±%
            
How far off the predicted magnitude was from the actual move. ±1.2% means the predicted % was on average 1.2 points away from reality. Lower is better.

          

          

            🔴
            Red + Small Variance
            
Dangerous combination. Model is correctly sizing the move but has the direction inverted. The stock likely behaves counter-cyclically — consider flipping its signal (e.g. defensive stocks that rise when VIX spikes).

          

          

            🟢
            Green + Small Variance
            
Ideal. Model is getting direction right and the magnitude is close. High confidence in predictions for this stock.

          

          

            🟡
            Green + Large Variance
            
Direction is right but magnitude is off. Useful for trade direction but don't rely on the predicted % size of the move.

          

          

            ⚪
            50–60% Accuracy
            
Marginal — close to random chance. Not enough data to trust this stock's predictions. Watch for improvement as more days accumulate.

          

        

      

      

        
Assumptions

        
The backtest does not assume you bought every stock in the watchlist. It only replays signals that cleared the model's buy threshold and then applies the app's simplified position-sizing rules.

        
It is also not using your exact real trades or proving you will reach the target. It is a model replay of selected signals over recent history, meant to test whether the signal logic had an edge.

P&L Simulator

            
Replays every buy signal through the position sizing model using actual returns, then runs 1,000 Monte Carlo simulations to project 12 months forward.

          

          
        

        

        

          
          

          
          

          

            
What this means

            
Monte Carlo does not predict one exact future. It runs 1,000 possible 12-month paths using your recent win rate, average win/loss size, and trading frequency, then shows a range of outcomes.

            
The 10th percentile is a cautious case, the 50th percentile is the middle outcome, and the 90th percentile is a strong upside case. The target probability shows how often those 1,000 simulated paths finished above your goal.

          

          

            
Simulator assumptions

            
The simulator assumes you keep following the model's qualifying buy signals and position-sizing rules. It does not assume you buy every recommendation, and it does not track your exact real brokerage execution.

            
Reaching the target here is a probability estimate under those assumptions, not a promise that a string of marginal gains will compound exactly into the target amount.

Setup Guide

        

          

            1
            

              Maintain your watchlist and holdings
              
BUY alerts draw from the strongest recommendation candidates, while SELL alerts focus on stocks you already hold. Keep your holdings and watchlist current so the alert ranking stays relevant.

            

          

          

            2
            

              Configure Email (Gmail)
              
In your .env file set SMTP_USER, SMTP_PASS (Gmail App Password), and ALERT_EMAIL. Get an App Password at myaccount.google.com/apppasswords.

            

          

          

            3
            

              Configure SMS via Twilio (optional)
              
Sign up free at twilio.com, then set TWILIO_ACCOUNT_SID, TWILIO_AUTH_TOKEN, TWILIO_FROM_NUMBER, TWILIO_TO_NUMBER in your .env file.

            

          

          

            4
            

              Restart the backend
              
After editing .env, restart uvicorn. Then click Send Preview Email or Send Test WhatsApp to verify.
